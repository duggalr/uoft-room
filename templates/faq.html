What's this site about?

Who's going to be in my CSC207 class this coming fall semester? Which students have done an internship at the WWF organization? Any other people on campus listen to the 'Highway Robbery' band? Who's trying to be on my basketball intramural team this semester? What are some interesting things other Uoft students are talking about?

What's weird about the current landscape of social media is broadcasting information at a global scale is extremely simple. Instagram and Tiktok have become massive central hubs to allow creators to share information their followers would enjoy. However, we seem (at least I think) to be losing online social networks that are localized to an (offline)-community. It's kind of weird that simple questions like the ones mentioned above are not that easy to find answers to...

At the moment, the primary medium that serves as an 'online hub' for Uoft students to connect with each other is Discord or Reddit. Even though both mediums are great, they do lack the 'real-identity' aspect which I feel is important for certain types of online communities, especially a college campus. When (the)-facebook first started, the fact that it was exclusive to your college campus made it cool... students could have fun on the site but also, find and share relevant information with other students. (zuck (kinda) screwed it all up, didn't he?)

I quickly created this website as an experiment to see if students would join. The inital version is quite minimal and predicated more on creating individual profiles, connecting with other students on campus, and sharing relevant information. This project is also open-source so please feel free to contribute (needs some work on the UI as i'm sure you can tell...)
